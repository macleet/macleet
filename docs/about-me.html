# south korea

While born in South Korea, I left the country after only a year, headed straight to Hong Kong without an idea of the where I was going and, for that matter, who I was.

# hong kong

I do not hold many memories of Hong Kong, not due to its insignificance but rather purely due to my infancy. However, my parents seem to hold fond and bittersweet memories, and they seem to have filled the gaps in my memory. Oh, and I used to go by Tommy during this time.

# singapore

Out of all the countries I've lived in, Singapore is the one I lived in longest. Therefore, it is the most nostalgic. Here, my best friend was a North Korean, with whom I attended school. We shared many interests, one of which was Naruto, a popular anime series. Such a vibrant and chaotic mind, he introduced me to new activities like water skiing and gaming.

# new jersey

Across the Hudson river from New York City is a small suburb. Having lived in warmer climates for all my life, I was shook to my core by the Jersey cold. Other than that, I recall there being a group of kids from my school who rode bikes together around the neighborhood. Wanting to fit in, I pulled out my handy-dandy foldable bike to be among those with BMX bikes. I still had fun though.

# mumbai

Comfortably back in the heat, I moved to Mumbai (or Bombay). I would be lying if I said that I did not experience any culture shock. However, once I had settled in, I realized that the country and its people were warm and inviting. Significantly, the food I had in India is unforgettable, a perfect representation of the liveliness of its culture.

# seoul

Back home, I felt like an outsider. A Korean who spoke below-average Korean, unaware of the cultural and social norms of the country. However, I thankfully was able to adapt to the familiar but unfamiliar setting, and formed the most meaningful relationships of my life. Unrelated, but this was also a time when I realized the decisions I made had real weight and consequences.

# davis ca

During my time at UC Davis studying computer science, I consistently had doubts about my abilities to excel in the field. And, to an extent, I still do. However, I perhaps realize now that this seeming underconfidence comes with the field due to its fast-paced nature. Therefore, I'm currently striving to change my attitude from 'keeping up' to 'looking forward.'

# seattle wa

After a year of computer science, I began to seriously doubt my prospect in the field. To get more acquanted with myself and my ambitions, I moved to Seattle to be with my brother. Due to the COVID pandemic, the two years spent there is a blur, but I can say that I developed not only as a worker in a professional environment, but also as a person in general.

where to next?

Without an idea of the where I am going and who I really am, I am currently looking for my next destination, where I can meet new people, make new memories, adapt to new surroundings, share new ideas, eat new food, experience different cultures, learn new things, and, most importantly, find my new self.
